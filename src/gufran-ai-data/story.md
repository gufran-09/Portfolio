# Gufran Ahmed — Engineering Story

Gufran Ahmed is a Computer Science student at Vardhaman College of Engineering, Hyderabad, with a growing focus on software engineering, backend systems, and AI-powered applications.

He started by building a strong base in frontend development, especially with React and modern web tooling, and gradually expanded into full-stack engineering by working with Node.js, databases, and backend application design. Over time, his interests moved beyond only building interfaces and toward understanding how complete products are structured — from APIs and databases to deployment workflows and AI integrations.

A defining part of his journey has been the shift from student-only coursework to practical product and engineering work. Through projects and real-world engagements, he has worked on travel-product workflows, dashboard-style interfaces, Spring Boot applications, and AI-enabled systems such as a college FAQ chatbot built around retrieval and LLM workflows.

Gufran is particularly interested in building software that is useful, technically solid, and grounded in real use cases. His work reflects a mix of frontend execution, backend curiosity, product thinking, and a willingness to explore emerging AI tooling where it can add practical value.

As an engineer, he is currently strongest in full-stack web development, Spring Boot and backend learning, React-based product development, and building applications that combine traditional software engineering with AI-assisted workflows.
